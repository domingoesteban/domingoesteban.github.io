WHOLE-BODY TRAJECTORY GENERATION FOR A HUMANOID ROBOT

Domingo Esteban

Whole-body motion of a humanoid robot is still a difficult problem for the robotics community, because planning and control of motion in a hyper-redundant system as a humanoid is not easy. Within the many movements that can be generated, a whole-body walking is still complex; the definition of the trajectories that have to be executed by the different parts of the body, in such a way that the step is stable, it can be a tedious task.

Considering the above, my master thesis attempted to develop whole-body movement by the generation of trajectories in the operational space and the inverse kinematics control to generate the trajectory for the joints, concretely, first and second order algorithms were used.

The work was applied in TEO, a humanoid robot developed by the Carlos III of Madrid University. First, the definition of the configuration of TEO in the operational space and in the generalized coordinates space were proposed.

The thesis focused on the generation of trajectories necessary for whole-body walking considering a number of footprints. The generation of this footprints were done with the local gait axis algorithm. A pattern generator for the whole-body gait were also proposed and tested for different sequences of footprints.
